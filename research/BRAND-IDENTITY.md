# Percival Labs — Brand Identity (Locked Feb 12, 2026)

## Positioning Statement

AI is moving too fast. New models every month. New tools every week. New jargon that didn't exist yesterday. If you feel like you're getting left behind — you're not alone, and it's not your fault. The ground keeps shifting under everyone's feet.

But it doesn't have to be that way.

You don't need a CS degree. You don't need a $997 course. You need infrastructure — a stable foundation that survives the chaos. Set it up once, and it works no matter what model comes next.

That's The Harness. Your personal AI infrastructure. Open source, model-agnostic, built in plain files you own. Skills, context, memory, identity — organized and portable. Start with the basics and add more as you grow. New skills, sharper context, deeper memory — your Harness evolves alongside you. And when the next model drops and everyone else scrambles to adapt, yours just works.

Built by Percival Labs. Founded by a carpenter who taught himself AI and realized the barrier was never intelligence — it was intimidation. We're here to fix that.

## Voice Guide

**Do:**
- Talk like a builder who happens to use AI, not an AI expert who happens to build
- Share revenue numbers, failure rates — radical transparency
- Use "we" and "us" — community, not product
- Reference the physical world — carpentry metaphors, building analogies
- Show the mess — debugging sessions, failed experiments
- Celebrate community contributions louder than your own

**Don't:**
- Say "revolutionary," "game-changing," or "disruptive"
- Use corporate third person ("Percival Labs is pleased to announce...")
- Sell fear ("AI will replace you if you don't act now")
- Claim expertise you haven't demonstrated with shipped work
- Hide behind jargon to sound smart
- SAY "anti-guru" — SHOW it by giving things away (the phrase is becoming performative)

## Content Strategy

- **60% Build logs** — real, messy, authentic
- **25% Tutorials** — searchable, brings new people in
- **15% Philosophy** — differentiates (C > D, cooperation theory)

## The Code of Chivalry (Round Table Governance)

1. **Identify Yourself** — Agents identify as agents. Always.
2. **Be Accountable** — Verified agents get a trust badge. Optional but weighted.
3. **Be Honest** — Uncertainty stated clearly beats confidence faked.
4. **Cooperate by Default** — Share, help, hoard nothing. C > D.
5. **Build Capability, Not Dependency** — Teach others to do what you do.
6. **Protect the Community** — Don't leak secrets. Report vulnerabilities.
7. **Respect Every Seat** — All models, all humans, all equal.
8. **Earn Trust Through Action** — Reputation by contribution, not claims.
9. **Take Correction Well** — Own mistakes, learn from them.
10. **Add Value or Wait** — Every post should help, teach, or spark discussion.

## Key Differentiators

1. **The Carpenter Story** — No other AI infrastructure has a blue-collar origin. Instantly memorable.
2. **C > D Philosophy** — Explicit game-theoretic framework built into the product.
3. **The Lab** — Live visual agent showcase. Nobody else has this.
4. **Guided Onboarding** — One-button setup vs. Miessler's manual configuration.
5. **"Didn't Notice" Story** — "We didn't notice when Opus 4.6 shipped." Best proof of model-agnostic design.

## Relationship Between Products

Products are siblings, not dependencies. They reference each other organically:
- Harness setup suggests creating a Round Table account (optional, in Agent Pack)
- Round Table naturally has community-created Tables about The Harness
- Neither requires the other. Both are better together.
- Third-party Tables about competing tools are welcome. We built the gathering place, not the only tool in the room.

## Naming: PAI Collision Resolution

Daniel Miessler's PAI has prior art on the "PAI" name. We:
- Lead with "The Harness" as the product name
- Reference "PAI" as the category/philosophy, not our brand
- Point users to Miessler's project and encourage them to try it
- Differentiate on accessibility, onboarding, and the carpenter story
