### Washington State Regulatory Context

**Key WA DFI Facts for HODL Invoice Analysis**:
- WA defines "virtual currency storage" as storing access to virtual currency owned by another
- Virtual currency storage does NOT require MTL if another person owns the stored currency
- Payment processing exemption does NOT extend to virtual currency payment processing
- WA requires $10K-$550K bond for MTL depending on transaction volume
- Contact: banks@dfi.wa.gov, [dfi.wa.gov/fintech/industry](https://dfi.wa.gov/fintech/industry)

### Patentability Assessment: Construction-Derived Milestone-Gated Contract System

**Key Considerations**:

1. **Alice Corp v. CLS Bank Risk**: The 2014 Supreme Court decision found that an "electronic escrow service" patent was ineligible as an abstract concept. Your construction-derived milestone-gated contract system using HODL invoices has direct parallels to escrow -- this is a known danger zone.

2. **How to Survive Alice**: The USPTO looks for inventions that alter computing behavior, not just task assignment. Your patent would need to demonstrate:
   - Technical improvement to computing infrastructure (not just "doing contracts on blockchain")
   - Specific implementation details of HODL invoice integration
   - Novel three-party verification mechanism as a technical architecture, not just a business process
   - How the system improves on existing payment channel technology

3. **Strategy**: File a provisional ($65 USPTO micro-entity fee + attorney costs) to establish priority date, then spend 12 months building usage data and refining claims before non-provisional filing.

4. **Recommended Approach**: Consult Rapacke Law Group or COJK for a free/low-cost patentability assessment call before committing to filing.
