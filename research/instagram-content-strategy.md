## 1. Platform Analysis: How Instagram Differs

### Instagram vs X vs TikTok

| Dimension | X/Twitter | TikTok | Instagram |
|-----------|-----------|--------|-----------|
| **Discovery** | Algorithmic timeline + search | For You Page (all algorithmic) | Explore page + Reels tab + hashtags |
| **Content lifespan** | 12-48 hours | 24-72 hours (can resurface weeks later) | Feed posts: 24-48 hours. Reels: 7-30 days. Carousels: weeks (saves extend reach) |
| **Best for** | Hot takes, threads, debate | Curiosity-gap hooks, rapid reach | Visual storytelling, education, portfolio |
| **Audience intent** | Information, discourse, networking | Entertainment, quick learning | Inspiration, learning, aspiration |
| **Engagement style** | Quote tweets, replies, threads | Comments, shares, stitches | Saves, shares, DMs, carousel swipes |
| **Content format** | Text-first (images supplement) | Video-first (photos emerging) | Visual-first (all formats available) |

### What Works on Instagram for Builder/Tech Content

**Carousels are king.** Instagram carousels get 1.4x more reach and 3.1x more engagement than single images. For educational content specifically, carousels get the highest save rate of any format -- and saves are the most powerful ranking signal. Every save tells the algorithm "this is worth showing to more people."

**Reels are the discovery engine.** 50% of time on Instagram is spent watching Reels. New accounts get algorithmic favor on Reels for the first 30-60 days -- Instagram wants you addicted to posting. Reels reach non-followers at 2-3x the rate of feed posts.

**Stories build loyalty.** They don't grow your audience, but they keep the audience you have. Daily Stories from a builder/maker account create the "following along" feeling. Low-effort, high-connection.

**What doesn't work:**

- Stock photo aesthetics. The grid-obsessed era is over. Instagram's algorithm now rewards engagement over visual consistency.
- Screenshots of tweets. Lazy cross-posting. IG users hate seeing content that obviously came from another platform.
- Long captions with no visual hook. People scroll fast. The image/first slide has to stop the thumb before the caption does anything.
- Overly polished content from builder accounts. If you're sharing a debugging session and it looks like a Squarespace ad, people sense the mismatch.
- Hashtag spam in the caption body. Instagram's own guidance: put hashtags at the end or in a comment.

### Where Instagram Fits in the PL Ecosystem

```
TikTok (discovery, curiosity)
   |
   |  "Wait, what is this?"
   |
   v
Instagram (visual portfolio, education)
   |
   |  "This person is legit. Let me learn more."
   |
   v
X/Twitter (depth, community, authority)
   |
   |  "I want to try this."
   |
   v
percival-labs.ai (product, signup, beta)
```

Instagram's role is the **visual middle layer**. TikTok hooks curiosity. Instagram proves credibility through a curated body of work. X goes deep. The website converts.

Someone who finds you on TikTok checks your Instagram to see if you're real. Someone who follows you on X might not use X daily but checks Instagram every morning. Instagram is where the "body of evidence" lives.

## 2. Content Pillars (Mapped to Instagram Formats)

### The 60/25/15 Ratio on Instagram

| Pillar | % | Instagram Formats | Examples |
|--------|---|-------------------|----------|
| **Build Logs** | 60% | Reels (screen recordings), Stories (daily WIP), Carousels (before/after breakdowns) | "Today's debugging session in 7 slides," "Watch me set up AI memory in 60 seconds," terminal screenshots with annotations |
| **Tutorials** | 25% | Carousels (step-by-step guides), Reels (quick how-tos), Guides (curated collections) | "Set up your AI context in 5 steps," "What AI memory actually looks like," "Engram setup walkthrough" |
| **Philosophy (C > D)** | 15% | Static posts (quote cards), Carousels (concept breakdowns), Reels (talking head or text overlay) | "Why we give everything away," "Cooperation vs defection explained," "The $997 course is a text file" |

### Pillar Details

#### Build Logs (60%)

This is the core. Unpolished, real, ongoing documentation of building AI tools as a carpenter. Not tutorials -- raw process.

**Carousel build logs:**
- 7-10 slides showing a real problem, the mess of solving it, and the result
- Annotated screenshots (arrows, circles, handwritten-style text overlays)
- Slide 1: The problem ("My AI forgot everything again")
- Slides 2-7: The journey (what I tried, what broke, what worked)
- Final slide: The result + one-line takeaway

**Reel build logs:**
- 30-60 second screen recordings of actual work
- Sped-up terminal sessions with text overlay narration
- "POV: debugging at midnight because you're almost there"
- Raw, imperfect, real

**Story build logs:**
- Daily WIP updates: "Working on Vouch scoring today"
- Quick polls: "Which feature should I build next?"
- Screenshots of code/terminal with no editing
- "The desk right now" photos -- messy, authentic, human

#### Tutorials (25%)

Educational content designed to be saved and shared. The highest-value format for Instagram growth because saves compound reach over time.

**Carousel tutorials:**
- 5-10 slides, each making one clear point
- Slide 1: Bold claim or question ("Your AI forgets you every conversation. Here's the fix.")
- Middle slides: Step-by-step, one action per slide
- Final slide: Summary + CTA ("Save this for later. Link in bio to try it.")
- Design: Dark background, cyan accents, clean typography. Consistent but not rigid.

**Reel tutorials:**
- 30-90 seconds
- "How to [specific thing] in under a minute"
- Screen recording with voiceover or text overlay
- Hook in first 2 seconds: show the result, then show how

**Guide collections (Instagram feature):**
- Group related carousel tutorials into Guides
- "Getting Started with AI" guide
- "Understanding AI Context" guide
- These become evergreen, searchable resources on your profile

#### Philosophy / C > D (15%)

The ideas that differentiate Percival Labs from every other AI account. These posts don't teach -- they provoke thought and build tribe identity.

**Static posts (quote cards):**
- Single image, dark background, one sentence
- "We build companions, not crutches."
- "The barrier was never intelligence. It was intimidation."
- "A $997 course for information that should be free. That's the defection we're fighting."
- Carpenter metaphors: "You don't need a master's in architecture to build a shed. You just need to start."

**Carousel philosophy:**
- 5-7 slides breaking down one concept
- "What is C > D?" explained with carpentry analogies
- "Why we open-source everything" -- the business case for generosity
- "The guru problem in AI education" -- calling it out with specifics

**Reel philosophy:**
- Talking head or text-over-B-roll
- 30-60 seconds max
- Strong opening statement, brief support, memorable close
- "Everyone's selling you a map. We're just showing you the trail."

## 3. Format Specifications

### Feed Posts (Square or Portrait)

| Spec | Value |
|------|-------|
| **Recommended size** | 1080 x 1350 px (4:5 portrait) |
| **Alternate size** | 1080 x 1080 px (1:1 square) |
| **File format** | JPG or PNG, max 30 MB |
| **Caption length** | Up to 2,200 characters (aim for 150-300 for most posts, longer for tutorials) |
| **Text overlay** | Keep within center 80% of image (Instagram crops edges on some views) |
| **Font size** | Minimum 24pt for body text, 36pt+ for headlines |

**Design rules:**
- Dark backgrounds (`#0A0E1A` or close) with cyan (`#00D4FF`) and amber (`#FFB800`) accents
- Keep text overlays high-contrast -- white or light gray on dark
- No more than 40 words per slide on carousels
- Leave breathing room -- Instagram is visual-first, don't pack slides

### Carousels

| Spec | Value |
|------|-------|
| **Slide count** | 2-10 slides (7-10 is optimal for saves and engagement) |
| **Recommended size** | 1080 x 1350 px (4:5 portrait, all slides same dimensions) |
| **File format** | JPG or PNG per slide |
| **First slide** | Hook -- must work as a standalone thumbnail in the feed |
| **Last slide** | CTA -- "Save this," "Share with someone who needs this," or "Link in bio" |

**Carousel design framework:**

```
Slide 1: HOOK (bold claim, question, or surprising fact)
Slide 2: CONTEXT (why this matters, the problem)
Slides 3-8: CONTENT (one point per slide, visual + 1-2 sentences)
Slide 9: SUMMARY (key takeaway in one line)
Slide 10: CTA ("Save for later" + "Follow @percivallabs for more")
```

### Reels

| Spec | Value |
|------|-------|
| **Dimensions** | 1080 x 1920 px (9:16 vertical) |
| **Length** | 15-90 seconds (30-60 is the sweet spot for educational content) |
| **Safe zones** | Top 15% and bottom 25% may be covered by UI -- keep key text in center 60% |
| **Captions** | Always add text captions (70%+ watch without sound) |
| **Cover image** | Custom 1080x1920 thumbnail that reads well as a 4:5 crop on the grid |

**Reel structure:**
- Hook: 0-3 seconds (grab attention, state what they'll learn/see)
- Content: 3-50 seconds (deliver the value)
- CTA: Last 5-10 seconds (follow, save, check link in bio)
- Use trending audio when it makes sense, but never force it

### Stories

| Spec | Value |
|------|-------|
| **Dimensions** | 1080 x 1920 px (9:16 vertical) |
| **Duration** | Photo: 5 seconds. Video: up to 60 seconds per segment |
| **Safe zones** | Keep key content in center 70% (username top, reply bar bottom) |
| **Interactive elements** | Polls, questions, quizzes, sliders -- use 2-3x per week minimum |
| **Highlights** | Curate into permanent categories on profile |

**Story highlight categories:**
- "Build Log" -- ongoing project updates
- "Start Here" -- intro to Percival Labs and Engram
- "Tutorials" -- saved tutorial stories
- "C > D" -- philosophy posts
- "About" -- who Alan is, the carpenter story

| Task | Time | Frequency |
|------|------|-----------|

Dedicate one session per week (Saturday morning or Sunday evening) to batch-create the next week's content:

1. Review what performed well on X and TikTok that week
2. Identify 2-3 pieces to adapt for Instagram
3. Create carousel slides in one sitting (Canva, Figma, or a simple template)
4. Record 1-2 Reels while in "content mode"
5. Schedule everything using Instagram's native scheduler or Later/Buffer
6. Stories are the only daily-creation format -- everything else is pre-batched

## 6. Cross-Posting Optimization

### X Threads to Instagram Carousels

X threads are the primary content source for Instagram carousels. But cross-posting requires transformation, not screenshots.

**Process:**

1. Take the X thread (6-12 tweets)
2. Distill each tweet into one visual slide (1-2 sentences max per slide)
3. Add visual design: dark background, brand colors, clean typography
4. Replace Twitter-specific formatting (@ mentions, quote tweets) with Instagram-native language
5. Add a hook slide at the beginning that works as a standalone feed thumbnail
6. Add a CTA slide at the end

**What to change:**
- Remove "1/" threading notation
- Replace "@handle" mentions with "a developer" or the person's name
- Add visuals (screenshots, diagrams, icons) that the text-only thread didn't need
- Make slide 1 a bold visual hook, not the thread's opening tweet verbatim
- Carousel should stand alone -- someone should understand it without reading the thread

**What to keep:**
- The core argument structure
- Specific numbers and data points
- The builder voice and carpenter metaphors
- The CTA pointing to percival-labs.ai

**Example transformation:**

X thread tweet:
> "Tech bros: 'I built a multi-agent RAG pipeline with vector embeddings.' Translation: 'I put text files in a folder and my AI reads them.' That's literally it."

Instagram carousel slide:
- Dark background
- Top half: "What they say:" in small gray text, followed by "multi-agent RAG pipeline with vector embeddings" in jargon-heavy styling
- Bottom half: "What it actually is:" in small gray text, followed by "text files in a folder" in clean, friendly typography
- Amber accent line separating the two halves

### TikTok Slideshows to Instagram

TikTok slideshows translate almost directly to Instagram carousels. The format is native to both platforms.

**Process:**

1. Export TikTok slide images (or use the originals before uploading to TikTok)
2. Resize from TikTok's dimensions to 1080x1350 (Instagram's optimal carousel size)
3. Remove any TikTok-specific UI references
4. Adjust text safe zones (TikTok and Instagram have different UI overlay areas)
5. Post as carousel, not Reel

**TikTok videos to Instagram Reels:**
- Remove TikTok watermark (re-export from original, never post watermarked content -- Instagram suppresses it)
- Adjust aspect ratio if needed (both are 9:16, usually fine)
- Add Instagram-native captions (don't rely on TikTok's auto-captions)
- Change CTA from "link in bio" (same) but update any TikTok-specific language

### Cross-Platform Calendar Sync

| Source Platform | Instagram Adaptation | Delay |
|----------------|---------------------|-------|
| X thread posted Monday | Instagram carousel Tuesday-Wednesday | 1-2 days |
| TikTok slideshow posted Tuesday | Instagram carousel or Reel Thursday | 2-3 days |
| X proof post (screenshot/reaction) | Instagram Story + static post | Same day (Stories) or next day (post) |
| TikTok viral hit | Instagram Reel (re-export from original) | 1-3 days |

**The 1-3 day delay is intentional.** It prevents your followers who are on multiple platforms from seeing the same thing twice in the same scroll session. It also lets you see what resonated on the source platform before investing in the Instagram adaptation.

### What NOT to Cross-Post

- Screenshots of tweets (looks lazy, Instagram users resent it)
- TikTok videos with watermarks (Instagram suppresses reach by up to 80%)
- Content that references platform-specific features ("this thread," "stitch this," "quote tweet this")
- Hot takes that depend on X's real-time context (by the time it hits Instagram, the moment has passed)

## 7. Growth Tactics

### Tactic 1: Reels for Algorithmic Discovery

Instagram gives new accounts a Reels boost for the first 30-60 days. Use this window aggressively.

**Execution:**
- Post 1-2 Reels per week minimum during the first 60 days
- Keep them 30-60 seconds (completion rate matters -- shorter = higher completion = more reach)
- First 3 seconds must hook. Show the result first, then explain how.
- Use trending audio when it fits naturally. Check the Reels audio library for rising tracks.
- Add text captions always -- 70%+ watch muted

**What performs in our niche:**
- Before/after screen recordings ("Here's my AI without context. Here's my AI with Engram.")
- "POV: you just set up AI memory for the first time" (relatable + aspirational)
- Quick explainers with text overlay on B-roll of workspace/tools/terminal
- The carpenter origin story told in 60 seconds (this will be the breakout piece -- the contrast is inherently compelling)

### Tactic 2: Carousel Saves = Algorithmic Gold

Saves are the most powerful engagement signal on Instagram. Carousels get saved at 2-3x the rate of single images because they contain reference-worthy information.

**Execution:**
- End every carousel with "Save this for later" (explicitly ask -- it works)
- Make carousels genuinely useful enough that people want to come back to them
- Tutorial carousels and "how-to" content gets saved at the highest rates
- Create "mini-guide" carousels that serve as complete references on one topic
- Track save rate per post and double down on topics that get saved most

### Tactic 3: Instagram Collabs for Cross-Audience Exposure

The Collab feature lets two accounts co-author a single post that appears on both profiles. It's the most efficient way to reach a new audience on Instagram.

**Target collab partners (in order of priority):**
1. **Other builders/makers in the "build in public" space** -- shared audience, non-competitive
2. **AI educators who aren't gurus** -- people teaching AI honestly, not selling courses
3. **Tradespeople who use technology** -- carpenters, electricians, plumbers who integrate tech into their work
4. **Small business owners using AI** -- the "regular person does impressive thing" angle
5. **Ian's accounts** (if applicable) -- family cross-promotion for initial boost

**How to approach:**
- Engage with their content genuinely for 2-3 weeks first (comment, share to Stories)
- DM with a specific collab idea, not a generic "let's collab"
- Offer to do the work -- "I'll create the carousel, you just review and approve"
- Make it about their audience, not yours

### Tactic 4: Consistent Posting Schedule

Instagram's algorithm rewards accounts that post consistently at predictable times. Erratic posting gets punished.

**Execution:**
- Pick 3-4 posting days and stick to them for at least 90 days
- Post at the same general time each day (within a 2-hour window)
- Best times for tech/education content: 8-9 AM, 12-1 PM, or 7-8 PM (audience's local time -- test and adjust)
- Use Instagram's native scheduler (Professional accounts have this built in) or a tool like Buffer/Later
- Never go more than 3 days without a feed post during the first 90 days

### Tactic 5: Strategic Comment Engagement

Commenting on larger accounts' posts is the cheapest growth tactic on Instagram. A thoughtful comment on a 100K-follower account's post gets seen by thousands.

**Execution:**
- Identify 10-15 accounts in adjacent niches (AI education, maker community, indie hackers, tech for normal people)
- Comment on their new posts within the first 30 minutes (early comments get pinned to the top)
- Comments must add value -- never "Great post!" or emoji spam
- Share a related insight, respectfully disagree with nuance, or add a specific example
- 5-10 genuine comments per day, max 15 minutes
- Do not comment with the intent to promote. Comment with the intent to contribute.

**Target accounts for engagement (starting points):**
- Accounts in the #BuildInPublic space
- AI educators targeting non-technical audiences
- Open source advocates
- Indie hackers and bootstrapped founders
- Maker/DIY tech communities

### Tactic 6: Profile Optimization for Conversions

Every profile visit is a potential follower. The conversion happens in the first 3 seconds of looking at your profile.

**Optimize:**
- Bio (see Section 8 below)
- Profile picture: high-contrast, recognizable at small size. Alan's face or the PL logo -- test both, but face typically converts 20-30% better for personal brands.
- Grid appearance: first 9 posts should represent the content mix. Alternate carousels, Reels, and static posts so the grid looks varied.
- Pinned posts: Pin the 3 best-performing posts to the top of the grid. Update monthly.
- Highlights: Organized, named clearly, with custom covers in brand colors.

### Tactic 7: Instagram Stories Engagement Features

Stories have built-in interactive features that boost engagement metrics. Use them.

**Weekly interactive Story plan:**
- Monday: Poll ("What should I build this week?")
- Wednesday: Question sticker ("What's your biggest frustration with AI?")
- Friday: Quiz ("Can you guess what this code does?") or slider ("How confident are you with AI? 1-10")
- Responses to interactive Stories create DM threads, which signals relationship depth to the algorithm

### Metrics That Don't Matter (Ignore These)

- **Follower count alone.** 500 engaged followers who save your posts beats 5,000 ghost followers.
- **Likes.** The weakest engagement signal. Saves and shares drive reach. Likes are polite noise.
- **Grid aesthetics.** Nobody unfollows because your grid isn't perfectly color-coordinated. They unfollow because content stops being useful.
- **Posting "at the perfect time."** Consistency matters 10x more than timing. Post at a regular time and show up daily.

### Tracking Setup

Use Instagram's built-in Professional Dashboard (free with a Professional/Creator account):
- Content reach and engagement per post
- Follower demographics (age, location, active hours)
- Profile visits and link clicks

Supplement with a simple spreadsheet tracking weekly:
- Posts published (by format)
- Best-performing post (reach + saves)
- Engagement rate (calculated)
- New followers
- Bio link clicks

Review weekly. Adjust monthly. Don't over-analyze daily fluctuations.

### Warm-Up Protocol (First 5 Days -- No Posts)

Same principle as TikTok. Instagram suppresses accounts that go from zero to posting immediately. Spend 3-5 days behaving like a normal user:

1. Follow 20-30 accounts in AI education, maker, builder niches
2. Like 10-15 posts per day (not spam -- genuine interest)
3. Leave 3-5 thoughtful comments per day on relevant content
4. Watch Reels in your niche for 10-15 minutes (trains the algorithm on your content category)
5. Fill out your profile completely before engaging (people will check when they see your comments)
6. Day 5-6: Post your first 3 pieces of content within 24 hours, then settle into the weekly cadence

## 13. Design System (Quick Reference)

### Brand Colors on Instagram

| Element | Color | Hex |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Backgrounds | Dark navy | `#0A0E1A` |
| Primary accent | Cyan | `#00D4FF` |
| Warm accent / CTAs | Amber | `#FFB800` |
| Body text | White/light gray | `#F0F0F0` |
| Secondary text | Muted gray | `#8899AA` |
| Borders/dividers | Slate | `#1E2A3A` |

### Typography Rules

- Headlines: Bold sans-serif (Inter, Montserrat, or system equivalent). 36-48pt.
- Body: Regular weight sans-serif. 24-32pt.
- Code/terminal: Monospace (JetBrains Mono, Fira Code). 20-28pt.
- Max 40 words per carousel slide
- Line height: 1.4-1.6 for readability
- Left-aligned text (never centered for multi-line copy)

### Visual Consistency

- Every carousel starts with a bold hook slide that works as a thumbnail
- Consistent header/footer bar on slides (subtle, brand-colored)
- Screenshots get a subtle drop shadow and rounded corners
- Terminal screenshots: use a dark terminal theme that matches brand colors
- Annotate screenshots with arrows and circles in cyan or amber
- No stock photos. Ever. Real screenshots, real workspace, real terminal.

## What This Strategy Is NOT

- **Not a rebrand.** Same voice, same mission, same content philosophy. Just adapted for a visual platform.
- **Not "content marketing."** We're not manufacturing engagement. We're documenting real work and sharing what we learn. The content is the byproduct of building, not the other way around.
- **Not high-production.** Phone screenshots over designed graphics. Messy desks over studio setups. The authenticity IS the strategy.
- **Not audience-chasing.** We post for the curious person who stumbles on one carousel and thinks "wait, I could actually do this?" That's the person we're here for.
- **Not guru content.** Never "5 steps to master AI." Always "here's what I tried, here's what happened, here's what I learned."
