# Engram Content Marketing Strategy

**Platforms:** X/Twitter + TikTok
**Voice:** Alan Carroll — carpenter, builder, anti-guru
**Audience:** Curious normal people who think real AI power isn't for them
**Goal:** Build attention → demonstrate possibility → invite to beta

---

## The Core Insight

There's a massive group of people watching AI happen to other people. They use ChatGPT to write emails or ask questions, and they assume that's the ceiling. Meanwhile, they see tech bros on Twitter building agents, writing code, talking about "context engineering" — and it feels like another world.

It's not. The gap between "chatbot user" and "AI power user" is smaller than anyone's telling them. But the people with audiences — the gurus, the course sellers — have every incentive to keep that gap feeling huge. Complexity is their business model.

Engram's content doesn't sell a product. It sells a realization: **you can do this too, and nobody needs to charge you $997 to learn how.**

---

## Platform Strategy

### Why X + TikTok Together

| Platform | Role | Strength |
|----------|------|----------|
| **X/Twitter** | Authority + community | Where AI conversations happen. Longer-form breakdowns land here. Direct engagement with the AI-curious crowd. |

## X/Twitter Strategy

### Account: @PercyAI (or Alan's personal — TBD)

### Content Mix

| Type | % | Purpose |
|------|---|---------|
| **Possibility openers** | 40% | Short tweets that crack open what's possible. Make people stop scrolling and think "wait, really?" |
| **Walkthroughs & breakdowns** | 30% | Long-form posts/articles showing the actual setup. Plain language. No jargon. Screenshots of real text files. |
| **Proof & reactions** | 20% | Before/after moments. Beta tester reactions. Screenshots of AI remembering something from months ago. |
| **Invitations** | 10% | Soft CTAs. "Trying something. Small group. DM if curious." Never hard-sell. |

### Posting Cadence

- **Daily:** 1-2 short-form tweets (possibility openers, hot takes)
- **2-3x/week:** 1 long-form post or thread (walkthrough, breakdown)
- **Weekly:** 1 proof post (screenshot, reaction, before/after)
- **As needed:** Soft invitations when beta spots open

### Short-Form Tweet Templates (Possibility Openers — 40%)

**Pattern 1: The reveal**
> "Most people use AI like a search engine with personality. That's like buying a car and only sitting in it."

> "Your AI forgets who you are every time you start a new conversation. Mine hasn't forgotten in 4 months. Same AI. Different setup."

> "The AI companies want you to keep re-explaining yourself every conversation. That's not a limitation — it's a design choice. And you can override it."

**Pattern 2: The contrast**
> "What most people do with AI: ask it to rewrite an email.
> What's actually possible: an AI that knows your business, your goals, your writing style, and helps you think.
> The gap between those two things is smaller than you think."

> "Tech bros: 'I built a multi-agent RAG pipeline with vector embeddings.'
> Translation: 'I put text files in a folder and my AI reads them.'
> That's literally it."

**Pattern 3: The anti-guru**
> "Nobody should be charging you $997 to learn how to use AI. Here's the whole thing: write down who you are and what you're working on. Put it where your AI can read it. That's 80% of it."

> "The AI education space runs on one trick: make simple things sound complicated, then sell the 'simplification.' I'm going the other direction."

**Pattern 4: The personal story**
> "I'm a carpenter. I build houses. I also built a personal AI system that manages my projects, writes content, and remembers everything about my work. Both things are true and neither required a CS degree."

> "6 months ago I was using ChatGPT to look up building codes. Now my AI knows every project I'm working on, my preferences, my constraints, and my goals. What changed wasn't the AI — it was how I set it up."

### Long-Form Post Templates (Walkthroughs — 30%)

**Template 1: "Here's my entire AI setup"**
- Open with: what people assume it looks like (code, servers, complexity)
- Show: what it actually looks like (text files in a folder, screenshots)
- Explain: what each piece does in plain English
- Close: "You could set this up in 20 minutes. No code."

**Template 2: "The thing nobody tells you about AI memory"**
- The problem: every new conversation starts from zero
- Why companies don't fix this (they want your data on their servers)
- The alternative: local files that travel with you
- What this feels like in practice (specific example)

**Template 3: "I switched AI models and lost nothing"**
- The fear: you invest in one AI, then a better one comes out
- The reality: if your context is portable, switching is trivial
- Show the actual switch (before/after with different models)
- Implication: you're never locked in

**Template 4: "[Specific use case] walkthrough"**
- Pick a relatable scenario (managing a side project, planning a business, research)
- Show exactly how the AI handles it with context vs without
- Screenshots, real files, real output
- "This took me 10 minutes to set up"

### Proof Posts (20%)

- Screenshots of AI referencing something from weeks/months ago
- Beta tester quotes: "I didn't know this was possible"
- Side-by-side: same question to vanilla ChatGPT vs Engram-equipped AI
- Metrics once we have them: "50 people tried this. Here's what they said."

### Invitations (10%)

- "Building something for people who are curious about AI but feel locked out of the 'real' stuff. Small beta group. If that's you, DM me."
- Never "BUY NOW" or "LIMITED SPOTS." Always "if you're curious."
- Route through Ian's network first. X invitations come after the first cohort validates.

## TikTok Strategy

### Format: Photo Slideshows (Carousels)

TikTok is pushing carousels hard — 2.9x more comments, 2.6x more shares than video. AI-generated images aren't needed here. **Screen recordings and screenshots are the content.** This is real, not manufactured.

### The 6-Slide Formula (Adapted)

| Slide | Purpose | Engram Version |
|-------|---------|----------------|
| 1 | Hook with text overlay | "I showed my friend what AI actually does. She said 'that's not real.'" |
| 2 | The problem | Screenshot of ChatGPT: "I don't have access to previous conversations" |
| 3 | The discovery | Simple text file on screen: "This is my entire AI setup. It's a text file." |
| 4 | Transformation 1 | Screenshot: AI referencing personal context perfectly |
| 5 | Transformation 2 | Screenshot: AI working on a real project with full context |
| 6 | CTA | "This took 10 minutes. No code. Link in bio." |

### Content Categories

**Category 1: "AI can do THAT?" (Discovery)**
Show something impressive that AI does with proper context. The wow factor. Make people think their ChatGPT is broken by comparison.

**Category 2: "Wait, it's that simple?" (Demystification)**
Pull back the curtain. Show the actual text files. Show how un-technical it is. Kill the intimidation.

**Category 3: "The $997 course is a scam" (Anti-guru)**
Call out the AI education grift. Show what they're selling vs what it actually is. Position Engram as the free alternative to expensive nonsense.

**Category 4: "Regular person does [impressive thing]" (Social proof)**
Alan's story. Beta testers' stories. A parent using AI to manage their family schedule. A small business owner using it to run operations. Normal people, extraordinary results.

### Hook Formula

From Om Patel's framework — adapted for our audience:

`[trusted person] + [skepticism/surprise] + [you showed them] + [their reaction]`

Examples:
- "My wife asked what I was doing on my laptop at midnight. I showed her. She made me set it up for her too."
- "My brother runs a media company. I showed him my AI setup. He goes 'how is nobody talking about this?'"
- "A friend told me AI is just a fad. I showed him what mine does. He hasn't stopped texting me questions since."
- "My coworker said 'AI can't actually help with real work.' I shared my screen for 30 seconds. He went quiet."

### Production Notes

- **No AI-generated images.** Real screenshots, real screen recordings, real text on screen. Authenticity is the whole brand.
- **"Messy and real > perfect."** Phone screenshots over polished graphics. Casual text overlays over designed templates. It should look like someone excited to share something, not a marketing team.
- **Audio:** Add trending audio manually before posting. 10x reach difference. 60 seconds per post.
- **Text overlay specs (TikTok):** 6.5% image height, 30% from top (status bar eats top 10%, UI covers bottom 20%). Line breaks every 4-6 words. Full hook on slide 1.

### Posting Cadence

- **3x/day minimum** during growth phase (Om's recommendation — TikTok rewards volume)
- **Week 1-2:** Learning phase. Post variety, watch what hits. Expect hundreds to low thousands of views.
- **Week 3-4:** Double down on winning formats. Expect 10K-50K on good posts.
- **Month 2+:** Compounding. 50K-100K+ on consistent winners.

### Warm-Up Protocol (Critical)

Before posting anything:
1. Scroll AI/tech TikTok for 2-3 days
2. Like sparingly (don't spam)
3. Follow accounts in the niche
4. Comment genuinely on a few posts
5. THEN start posting

Skipping this = shadowban = 0 views. Non-negotiable.

### Diagnostic Framework (From Alex Lukianov's Comment)

| Views | Diagnosis | Fix |
|-------|-----------|-----|
| 0-200 | Hook failure | Rewrite slide 1. The opening line isn't stopping the scroll. |
| 200-2K | Story not holding | Slides 2-5 aren't compelling. Tighten the narrative. |
| 2K-10K | Weak engagement | People watch but don't comment/share. Add a question or controversial take. |
| 10K+ | Working | Analyze what hit. Replicate the pattern. |

### Learning Loop

Track manually at first (spreadsheet), automate later:
- Which hooks got views?
- Which CTA style drove profile visits?
- Which content category performs best?
- Are views converting to X follows / bio link clicks?

Update the playbook weekly based on data. Don't guess — measure.

## What This Is NOT

- **Not a product launch.** We're not announcing Engram. We're sharing what's possible and letting people come to us.
- **Not guru content.** Never "5 steps to master AI." Always "here's what I do, here's how it works."
- **Not developer content.** Zero jargon. Zero code in posts. If code is visible in a screenshot, it's incidental, not the point.
- **Not manufactured scarcity.** No "only 10 spots left!" No countdown timers. Just genuine invitations.
- **Not polished.** Real screenshots. Real voice. Real person. The authenticity is the entire competitive advantage against every AI influencer with a ring light and a course to sell.
